# Portfolio video scripts

Three 2-minute scripts for the hero-video slots on `index.html`, `lycia.html`, and `science37.html`.

Pacing target: **150–160 words per minute** (conversational, not rushed). Each script is ~290–310 words. Read once for time before recording — natural pauses and screen pointing usually push it slightly past 2 minutes, which is fine.

## 3. Science 37 (`science37.html`) — Sr. Product Designer, Visit Operations Platform

**Length:** ~305 words · ~1:58

> Hi, I'm Harun. This is the Visit Operations Platform portfolio from Science 37.
>
> Visit Operations Platform is the scheduling system that powers Science 37's decentralized clinical trials. As Senior Product Designer, I led the end-to-end design — discovery research, concept validation, the shipped UI, and the product architecture behind it.
>
> The system coordinates three hundred and thirty mobile clinicians across forty-six home states, four active studies, and two hundred enrolled participants. Outcomes: thirty-one percent increase in scheduling efficiency, and fifty percent reduction in scheduling personnel.
>
> This page is a ten-minute tour, in three stops — the discovery research, the full case study, and the product architecture. Each stop opens the actual deliverable as a PDF.
>
> A few high-value sections to call out.
>
> The outcomes grid up top shows the four numbers that justify the work — the thirty-one percent, the fifty percent, the three-hundred-and-thirty-clinician roster broken out by role, and the four studies with their visit cadences.
>
> The product section walks six of the eleven shipped surfaces. The one I'd flag for any reviewer is Optimize Day — a 2-opt drive-time optimizer that treats a nurse's day as a Travelling Salesman instance anchored at her home address. Nearest-neighbor seed, 2-opt local search, before-and-after diff side by side. One click to accept the shorter route. That feature alone moved hours of back-office work each week.
>
> The Smart Match panel is the second I'd call out — one click pairs the right RN, CRC, and investigator with the right visit, filtered by license, study training, and drive distance from the clinician's home address.
>
> Below the product walk, an eleven-screenshot gallery of the shipped UI, and four bound PDF deliverables — discovery, concept validation, the full case study, and the architecture document.
>
> If you want the deepest read, the case study PDF walks every surface with the design rationale behind each decision.
>
> Email me at harun@lycia.app.

## Recording notes

- **Pace:** read at the pace you'd talk to a hiring manager — not slow, not rushed.
- **Numbers:** spell out percentages and counts naturally ("thirty-one percent," "three hundred and thirty"). Already written that way above.
- **B-roll cues** *(optional)*: when you say "this page is structured as a ten-minute tour," the screen can cut to the tour section anchored on each page. When you mention a specific PDF, cut to that PDF cover.
- **Outro:** all three end with the email — keeps the call to action consistent across the videos.
- **Re-recording:** if a take runs over 2:15, trim the middle section, not the intro or the outro.
