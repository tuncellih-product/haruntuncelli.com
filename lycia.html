The thesis

One AI brain. Four verticals. A network that gets more valuable with each new participant.

Lycia AI is positioned today as a guest-facing autonomous agent. The next move is to make it a two-sided AI-mediated commerce network: hotels deploy the agent on the demand side, local venues join on the supply side, and commission flows in both directions on every recommendation the AI makes.

🏨

Hotels

Demand side. Deploy Lycia. Earn referral commissions on outbound recommendations to the network.

🍽

Restaurants

Supply side. Receive inbound diners from hotel guests + run their own AI for direct customers.

🎵

Music venues

Supply side. Sell tickets and fill weeknight shows via hotel-guest referrals + own AI.

🚤

Activities

Supply side. Tours, water sports, wine, wellness. Hotel-guest bookings + own AI.

The expansion thesis (this portfolio): Lycia AI today requires the hotel to already have a PMS — which gates the long tail of independent 5–50-room hotels who don't have one. The concept demo is an AI-native PMS bundled with the agent, designed to close that gate and unlock the supply-side demand for the network. Read the strategy thesis for the full argument.

The concept demo

An AI-native PMS for independent hotels.

The expansion plan, in working software. The PMS is the operational layer Lycia AI's overlay model can't reach — staff inbox, scheduling, tasks, rooms, rates, integrations. Built AI-first: auto-drafted replies, sentiment-driven escalation, multi-staff auto-assign with on-duty filtering and load balancing. Real cross-page data: every entity on a store factory, persisted to localStorage.

The interactive demo is being prepared for public deployment. Below is a walkthrough of what was built — six surfaces of an AI-first PMS, shipped as a working static front-end (~25K lines of code, custom design system, store-factory architecture). The live URL will be added here once it's deployed; source available on request.
▶ Demo surface

Home

Top-of-funnel landing. Hotel overview, daily briefing, navigation into every operational surface.

▶ Demo surface

AI-first inbox

Auto-drafted replies, AI category pills, sentiment indicator, escalation banner. Auto-assign to on-duty, eligible staff, load-balanced.

▶ Demo surface

Automations

Rules that route AI-detected requests to the right team. The other half of the multi-staff auto-assign behavior.

▶ Demo surface

Integrations

~30 connector cards across 9 categories: OTAs, payments, comms, accounting, door access, revenue mgmt, reviews, identity, smart home.

▶ Demo surface

Rate management

Per-suite rate calendar with date-based overrides. The base for the future rate-recommendation slice.

▶ Demo surface

Employees

Staff directory with cross-page data: each employee's detail page shows real schedule + tasks pulled from scheduling and task stores.

Architecture note. The demo runs as a static front-end with localStorage persistence. Intentionally no backend — this is a concept demo for the strategy, not a shipping product. The architecture (per-entity store factory with uniform CRUD + subscribe API) is designed to swap to a real API when the bundle ships.
